Oct 31 - Met with Professor Hegedus to show him our design and gain feedback on our calculations. We determined that a total energy demand of 180 Watts assuming the device can be on for 4 hours throughout the day. Along with 21.4 amp/hr per day running through the battery at a system efficiency of 70%. We calculated a worst case for our system not having power for 3 days(too many clouds) to show that a 100 Amp hour battery is needed. All these calculations lead use to need 7.2 amps of current required from the array and 72 Watts. With this knowledge we will most likely look for 2 12 volt arrays in parallel to meet this need.

Week of 11/11:

Nov 12 - Researched the difference between MPPT and PWM charge controllers. Came to the conclusion we had more knowledge to construct a PWM charge controller.

Nov 14 - Researched the different types of MOSFETS needed to interact with pwm systems to safely charge the battery. Found that using a N channel MOSFET is useful for controlling the power flow while P channel MOSFET is useful for driving the load. Wanted to ensure the MOSFETS had low RDS(on) values.
